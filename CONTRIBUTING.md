# Project IMACE Contributor Guide

Project IMACE welcomes contributions from researchers, developers, writers, reviewers, and collaborators who support its open research mission.

This repository follows a structured contribution process designed to preserve clarity, attribution, research quality, and consistency across the Project IMACE ecosystem.

---

## 1. Scope of Contributions

Project IMACE accepts contributions in the following forms:

- research proposals  
- code  
- documentation  
- conceptual frameworks  
- experimental notes  
- reviews and critiques  
- infrastructure support  
- tooling and workflow improvements  

All contributions must align with the Project IMACE research direction and governance framework.

Refer to:
- [Research Scope](https://github.com/project-imace/charter/blob/main/RESEARCH_SCOPE.md)
- [Charter](https://github.com/project-imace/charter/blob/main/CHARTER.md)

---

Before contributing, you must review:

---

## 3. Issue Workflow

Project IMACE uses structured issue templates.

When opening an issue:

- select the appropriate issue template  
- follow the required structure  
- provide complete and relevant information  
- use the correct issue category  

Issue templates are used for:

- research proposals  
- experiment logs  
- theoretical questions  
- discussions  
- suggestions  
- bug reports  

Unstructured or incomplete issues may be declined.

---

## 4. Pull Request Workflow

All pull requests must follow the repository’s pull request template.

A pull request must:

- clearly describe the contribution  
- reference related issues  
- define scope and intent  
- include relevant context for review  

Pull requests that do not follow the template or workflow may be rejected.

---

## 5. Branching

Follow the repository-defined branching strategy.

Typical patterns include:

- `research/<topic>`  
- `docs/<topic>`  
- `feat/<topic>`  
- `fix/<topic>`  
- `experiment/<topic>`  

Do not commit directly to protected or main branches unless explicitly permitted.

---

## 6. Contribution Standards

All contributions must:

- align with Project IMACE scope and structure  
- be clearly written and documented  
- preserve attribution and origin  
- avoid plagiarism and misrepresentation  
- maintain academic and conceptual clarity  
- follow the Code of Conduct and Contributor Covenant  

Submissions that are unclear, misleading, or out of scope may be revised or declined.

---

## 7. Licensing

Project IMACE follows a **repository-level licensing model**.

Refer to:
- [Licensing Policy](https://github.com/project-imace/charter/blob/main/LICENSING.md)

Key principles:

- all approved licenses are explicitly defined in the licensing document  
- contributors agree to operate within those defined licensing options  
- licensing flexibility exists only within the documented framework  
- no contribution may be used to convert Project IMACE work into private or closed systems  
- licensing changes must follow the documented governance process  

This agreement ensures flexibility without compromising openness.

---

## 8. Code of Conduct and Community Standards

All contributors must follow:

- [Project IMACE Code of Conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md)  
- [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/)  

These define:

- respectful communication  
- responsible collaboration  
- non-discriminatory participation  
- constructive and professional conduct  

Violations may result in enforcement actions as defined by project governance.

---

## 9. Review Process

All contributions are reviewed based on:

- alignment with research scope  
- conceptual and technical quality  
- clarity and completeness  
- compliance with governance and policy  
- relevance to Project IMACE objectives  

Contributions may be accepted, revised, or declined.

---

## 10. Recognition

Contributors may be acknowledged through:

- commit history  
- documentation credits  
- release notes  
- contributor listings  

Recognition does not imply governance authority unless explicitly granted.

---

## 11. Summary

Project IMACE is an open research initiative built on structured collaboration, academic rigor, and transparent governance.

By contributing, you agree to:

- follow defined issue and pull request workflows  
- adhere to branching and documentation standards  
- comply with licensing policies  
- uphold the Code of Conduct and Contributor Covenant  

All contributions must support the integrity, openness, and research objectives of Project IMACE.
